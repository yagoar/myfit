How to capture a good scan

      
    

    


      

        
1 Prep the room & outfit

        
    
          
  • Pick a uniformly lit room — diffuse daylight or a couple of soft lamps. Avoid hard shadows or direct sun stripes.
    • 
          
  • Wear tight, single-colour fitted layers: leggings + tank top, or a swimsuit. Loose fabric = wrong silhouette.
    • 
          
  • Tie a contrast-colour elastic at your natural waist. Cyan or red works best against skin/dark clothing. The GUI's Waist band picker must match.
    • 
          
  • Hair up so the neck and shoulders are visible.
    • 
        

      


      

        
2 Set up the capture

        
    
          
  • Use Stray Scanner on an iPhone Pro / Pro Max (LiDAR required).
    • 
          
  • Enable depth + confidence recording in Stray's settings. Frame rate: 60 fps if available.
    • 
          
  • Open space: at least 2.5 m clear around you on all sides.
    • 
          
  • Have someone else hold the phone and walk around you — the operator's job is to keep the camera steady and the distance constant. Self-scanning with an outstretched arm also works but the radius wobbles more.
    • 
        

      


      

        
3 Pose

        
    
          
  • A-pose: arms relaxed away from the body at ~30°, palms forward, fingers gently splayed.
    • 
          
  • Feet hip-width apart, weight even, gaze straight ahead.
    • 
          
  • Breathe normally. Do not hollow the chest or suck in the waist.
    • 
          
  • Stand still for the entire sweep. Any sway shows up as fit error.
    • 
        

      


      

        
4 Sweep

        
    
          
  • Phone height ~1 m. Camera tilted slightly up.
    • 
          
  • Walk a full 360° circle around the person, ~1.2-1.5 m away. Keep distance constant.
    • 
          
  • Take ~30-45 s per loop. Do two loops: one at hip height, one at chest height.
    • 
          
  • Cover every angle. Pay extra attention to armpits, between the legs, under the chin.
    • 
          
  • Stop the recording while still pointed at the subject — don't pan away last second.
    • 
        

      


      

        
5 Transfer & run

        
    
          
  • Export the Stray capture folder (rgb.mp4, depth/, confidence/, odometry.csv, camera_matrix.csv) into data/captures/<name>/.
    • 
          
  • Paste that path into Stray folder.
    • 
          
  • Pick the waist colour matching your elastic (or none to fall back to SMPL-X anatomical waist).
    • 
          
  • Pick the pattern system. Aldrich = the 5th-edition size chart; dpm = dresspatternmaking.com drafting; Both writes both CSVs.
    • 
          
  • Hit Run scan. First-time runs take ~3-6 min on Apple Silicon. Watch the log for chamfer ≤ 0.0008 and "extracted" counts.
    • 
        

      


      

        
Common failure modes

        
    
          
  • Loose clothing → fit picks up fabric, not the body. Re-scan in tight layers.
    • 
          
  • Glossy / black materials → LiDAR returns are weak. Add light or change top.
    • 
          
  • Single loop only → upper-body fit suffers. Two loops at different heights matter.
    • 
          
  • Wrong elastic colour → waist override fails silently and falls back to anatomical waist. Check the log.
    • 
          
  • Moving subject → mesh shows ghosting. Stand still or shorten the sweep.
